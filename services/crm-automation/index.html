AI CRM Automation for B2B Revenue Teams

Turn your CRM from a graveyard into a tool that actually runs your sales process. We automate the data entry, the follow-up tasks, and the pipeline visibility that your team never has time to maintain manually.

The CRM Problem

Most CRMs are full of incomplete data, missed tasks, and deals that went nowhere because nobody followed up. That is not a people problem. It is a process problem.

Dirty data nobody trusts

If your team does not trust the CRM data, they stop using it. Contacts with missing fields, outdated job titles, and wrong company info are not just annoying. They make the whole system unreliable.

Follow-up tasks that never get created

When creating a follow-up task depends on a rep remembering to do it after every call and every email, some of them will not happen. The deals that fall through the cracks are often the ones worth saving.

No pipeline visibility

If your pipeline view only reflects what reps have remembered to update, you are not looking at your pipeline. You are looking at a partial picture that gives you false confidence or false panic.

Renewal and upsell opportunities missed

Existing clients are your easiest revenue. But if nobody is monitoring contract dates or flagging accounts that have gone quiet, those opportunities pass without a conversation ever happening.

What We Automate in Your CRM

We build automations that handle the repetitive work inside your CRM so the data stays clean, the follow-ups happen, and the pipeline reflects reality.

→
Data Enrichment

The system automatically fills missing contact and company fields using third-party data sources. Job title, company size, industry, LinkedIn profile, phone number. Your CRM stays accurate without anyone doing it manually.

→
Automatic Task Creation

When a deal moves to a new stage, when a call is logged, or when an email gets no reply after a set number of days, the system creates the right follow-up task automatically. Nothing gets missed because nothing depends on someone remembering.

→
Pipeline Hygiene

The system identifies stale deals, flags them to the rep, and prompts an update or a close. Your pipeline reflects reality rather than wishful thinking.

→
Renewal and Upsell Alerts

As contracts approach renewal dates, the system triggers an alert and starts a re-engagement sequence. Accounts that have gone quiet get flagged before they become a churn risk.

→
Automated Reporting

Deal stage summaries, activity reports, and revenue forecasts are generated and distributed automatically on a schedule. No more building the same spreadsheet every Friday.

Example Workflow

01

A New Deal Enters the Pipeline

A new contact is created in the CRM. The system enriches the record with missing data, creates a discovery call task for the rep, and sets a five-day follow-up reminder. If there is no activity after ten days, the system flags the deal to the rep's manager. If the deal moves to proposal stage, the system creates a new task set for that stage and removes the old ones. Every step is logged automatically.

02

An Existing Client Approaches Renewal

Ninety days before a contract end date, the system flags the account, creates a renewal task for the account manager, and starts a light-touch re-engagement email sequence. The account manager knows about it in advance. The client feels looked after. The renewal conversation happens before the client starts looking elsewhere.

Who This Is For

CRM automation works best for B2B businesses where sales cycle length, data quality, and consistent follow-up all have a direct impact on revenue.

Sales Teams Using HubSpot, Pipedrive, or Salesforce

If your team is already on a CRM but not getting the value from it because of data quality and inconsistent usage, automation fixes the process without requiring a new tool.

Businesses with Longer Sales Cycles

Revenue Leaders Who Want Accurate Forecasting

Frequently Asked Questions

Will automations make changes in our CRM without us knowing?

No. We build automations with the level of control you are comfortable with. That might mean automations create tasks and drafts that a human approves, or it might mean full automation for lower-risk actions like data enrichment. You decide the boundaries.

How long does setup take?

A focused CRM automation build typically takes two to four weeks depending on the complexity of your sales process and the number of integrations involved. We will give you a specific timeline after the discovery call.

Do we need to be on a premium CRM plan?

It depends on the CRM and what you want to automate. Some features require higher tier plans. We will tell you exactly what is needed before we start and will not recommend anything unnecessary.

Our CRM data is a mess. Do we need to clean it first?

Not always. Some of what we build will clean the data as part of the automation. We will assess the state of your data during discovery and tell you honestly what needs to happen before automation can be effective.

Can you integrate the CRM with our other tools?

Yes. CRM automation rarely lives in isolation. We commonly connect CRMs with outreach tools, calendar systems, Slack, reporting dashboards, and financial software. Whatever your stack looks like, we will work with it.
